About Me

I am a second year PhD student in Computing and Data Sciences at Boston University, working with Jonathan Huggins and Michael Dietze . I am broadly interested in scientific machine learning, Bayesian modeling, and uncertainty quantification, with the goal of developing new methodologies for environmental and ecological applications.

I am also a co-organizer of the Environment and Climate working group within the Mechanism Design for Social Good (MD4SG) research intitiative.

Previously, I worked as a research assistant at the Federal Reserve Bank of Boston. I received my B.S. in Quantitative Economics from Tufts University in 2018.
